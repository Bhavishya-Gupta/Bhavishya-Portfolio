Chemical Eng. | Jan–Apr 2025
Ni–Mo Catalyst Synthesis on Silica Supports for HDS
UGP | Mentor: Prof. Sri Sivakumar · IIT Kanpur
Objective

Develop and validate Ni–Mo catalysts supported on amorphous Al₂O₃ blended with different silica supports for hydrodesulfurization of refractory sulfur compounds.

Approach
  • Synthesized am-Al₂O₃ via thermal decomposition and prepared silica variants (MCM-41, SBA-15, KIT-6).
  • Prepared 87% am-Al₂O₃ + 13% silica support blends via attrition mixing; performed citric acid-assisted wet impregnation and calcination at 550°C (2°C/min ramp).
  • Standardized lab procedures: pelletization, sieving, centrifugation/acetone washing, sonication; validated reactor loading via vibration filling and helium + soap-bubble leak tests.
  • Automated stoichiometric scaling with MATLAB to compute reagent/support weights for any target catalyst mass and composition.
Outcome
  • Produced 7 reproducible am-Al₂O₃ batches and 5 catalyst compositions (15%, 20%, 30%, 35%, 50% loadings) + 3 silica-variant catalysts at 20% loading.
  • Delivered a reusable MATLAB tool eliminating manual stoichiometric errors and accelerating formulation planning.
  • Established documented, repeatable lab protocols enhancing experimental safety and consistency.
